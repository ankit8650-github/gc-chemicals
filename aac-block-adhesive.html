GC AAC BLOCK ADHESIVE

AAC Block Adhesive is a factory prepared blend of carefully selected raw materials, Portland cement and graded aggregates and polymers. Designed for use with water to produce high strength thixotropic mortar, for laying Aerated Light weight concrete, Fly ash bricks, cement hollow blocks, Cellular concrete blocks or smoothing over the block work surface in layers of up to 12mm thickness, that meet and exceed the requirements of National and International Standards

AVAILABLE COLORS: White , Ivory , Black , Grey , Teracota , Blue , Brown

Application & Usage Guidelines

1

Area of Applications

  • Apply a layer of GC AAC BLOCK ADHESIVE to the horizontal and vertical side surface of the panel/block using a 6mm toothed trowel. Apply the adhesive to one panel/block at a time. Position the AAC panel/block gently and use a rubber mallet for tapping and a spirit level to check for alignment.
  • Apply GC AAC BLOCK ADHESIVE in a thin layer (2 to 3 mm) to enable the continuity of the thermal insulation of the AAC blocks. Ensure the applied layer does not form a superficial film before fixing the block. If this occurs, apply a fresh new layer.
  • Leave a space of about 1–2 cm between the top of the block wall and the bottom of the slab or beam to avoid formation of cracks along the wall. The cavity should be filled with compressible material.
  • Plastering can be applied to AAC walls after 24 hours of fixing the panels/blocks.
2

Precautions

  • Product contains cement and reacts strongly alkaline with moisture — protect skin and eyes.
  • After contact, wash immediately with plenty of water.
  • After eye contact, seek medical advice immediately.
  • It is recommended to use protective gloves and goggles.
  • For further and complete information about the safe use of this product, refer to the latest version of the Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS).
3

Cleaning

  • lean tools and containers with water while mortar is fresh.
  • Do not apply GC AAC BLOCK ADHESIVE in rainy weather, high winds, or stormy weather.
  • Ensure that the first layer of block is fixed properly with perfect alignment.
  • During sunny season, cover the working area to prevent direct sun exposure.
  • Keep equipment cool and use normal water.
  • The possible humidity rise is expected on the floor; it is recommended to apply a proper damp course coating.
  • Spray the block wall with water before applying the plaster.
  • Keep a 1–2 cm gap at the top end of the wall below the slabs or beam, and fill the gap with compressible material.
4

Grout Cleaning

  • Within 35–45 minutes of grout filling, clean the tile joints with a clean cloth or sponge.
  • Wet the cloth or sponge lightly in water before cleaning.
  • Do not press too hard on the grouted area while cleaning.
  • Do not use any type of acid when cleaning the grout.
  • The hardened adhesive can be removed from tools mechanically.
  • Note: Use caution when polishing soft glazed tiles or polished stone.
5

How to Mixing

  • Before commencing grouting, ensure the adhesive bed has properly set.
  • Joints must be clean of all adhesive residue, dust, or debris.
  • Ensure a clean and well-ventilated workspace.
  • Wear gloves and safety glasses to protect skin and eyes.
  • Use a 2:1 ratio of resin to hardener.
  • Pour resin into a mixing container, add hardener, and stir thoroughly for at least 2–3 minutes.
  • Scrape the sides and bottom of the container to incorporate all components.
  • Stir gently to minimize air bubbles.
  • Be mindful of the epoxy's pot life; work quickly.
  • Use a brush or pour directly onto the surface.
  • Allow sufficient curing time before handling or disturbing the epoxy.
